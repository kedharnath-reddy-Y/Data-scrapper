HSBC Travel One Credit Card Review

The HSBC TravelOne Credit Card is a solid choice for travel lovers, especially if you enjoy booking flights, hotels, and other travel-related expenses through platforms like Yatra, EaseMyTrip, and Paytm. 

One of the best parts of this card is that you can convert your reward points into Airmiles, hotel points, or vouchers. So whether you're flying or staying in hotels, you have options on how to use your rewards. Plus, the bonus points for meeting spending targets are a nice little incentive to keep swiping.

If you're the type who spends a lot of time in airports, the complimentary lounge access (6 domestic and 4 international visits per year) will make you feel like you're living the high life — no more awkwardly sitting on the floor near a charging station.

But let's keep it real: this card isn't perfect. The foreign exchange markup is a bit on the higher side, so if you're planning to spend a lot abroad, you might feel the sting. Also, the card doesn't earn rewards on fuel, insurance, or tax payments, which means you'll be missing out on some of your regular expenses.

Who Should Get an HSBC TravelOne Credit Card?

The HSBC TravelOne credit card is ideal for those who:

  • Frequent travellers looking for travel-related benefits and rewards
  • People who spend on flights, hotels, and travel agencies
  • Those who want to earn reward points on everyday spending
  • Individuals seeking discounts on duty-free shopping and dining
  • Cardholders who enjoy access to airport lounges
  • Shoppers looking for discounts on travel and entertainment bookings
  • Those who can meet the income eligibility criteria

How to Apply for an HSBC TravelOne Credit Card?

  • Step 1: To apply, visit HSBC's official website 
  • Step 2: Make sure that you meet the eligibility criteria
  • Step 3: Click on "Apply Now" and follow the instructions

If you travel frequently, love earning reward points, and enjoy lounge access, the HSBC TravelOne Credit Card is a great pick. The ability to convert points into air miles and hotel stays makes it valuable for those who fly often. However, the high forex markup and annual fee might be a dealbreaker for some.

HSBC TravelOne Credit Card Eligibility

To apply for HSBC TravelOne Credit Card, you must:

  • Be between 18 - 65 years old
  • Have a minimum annual income of ₹4 lakh
  • Reside in Chennai, Gurgaon, Delhi NCR, Pune, Noida, Hyderabad, Mumbai, Bangalore, Kochi, Coimbatore, Jaipur, Chandigarh, Ahmedabad, or Kolkata

Documents Required for HSBC TravelOne Credit Card

  • Proof of Identity: PAN Card, Aadhaar card, Driver’s License, Passport, Voter’s ID, Overseas Citizen of India Card, Person of Indian Origin Card, Job card issued by NREGA, Letters issued by the UIDAI or any other government-approved photo ID proof.
  • Proof of Address: Aadhaar card, Driver’s License, Passport, Utility Bill not more than 3 months old, Ration Card, Property Registration Document, Person of Indian Origin Card, Job card issued by NREGA, Bank Account Statement, or any other government-approved address proof.
  • Proof of Income: Latest one or two salary slips (not more than three months old), latest Form 16, and the last 3 months’ bank statement.

HSBC Bank Credit Card News
29 Jun 2024, Saturday
1. RBI imposes Rs 29.6 lakh fine on HSBC for credit card calculation violations

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has fined The Hongkong and Shanghai Banking Corporation Limited (HSBC) Rs 29.6 lakh for failing to adhere to specific credit card operation guidelines.

This action, taken on 24 June 2024, addresses HSBC's non-compliance with the mandated calculation procedures for determining the minimum payment due on credit card accounts. The central issue cited by the RBI is HSBC's calculation method leading to "negative amortization," a situation where a cardholder's balance increases due to the applied interest and fees surpassing the minimum payments made, potentially trapping customers in a debt cycle.

The fine underscores the importance of abiding by RBI's credit card rules, which are designed to prevent debt spirals among users. HSBC customers are advised to review their statements for accuracy in minimum payment calculations and contact customer service for any discrepancies.
