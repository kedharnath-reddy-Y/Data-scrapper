Standard Chartered Digi Smart Credit Card Review

The Standard Chartered Digismart Credit Card is suitable for regular commuters, particularly those who prefer using Ola for their rides. This card provides a cashback of 15% on all rides, up to a maximum of Rs. 600 per month. It is an entry-level credit card that comes with a low monthly fee of Rs. 49. Additionally, it offers considerable discounts on various categories, such as travel, entertainment, shopping, and more. You can take advantage of amazing perks and discounts when you shop at Myntra, Grofers, INOX, Zomato, and Yatra.

Who Should Opt for Standard Chartered Digi Smart Credit Card? 

The Standard Chartered Digi Smart Credit Card is ideal for users looking for discounts and benefits on travel, dining, and movie expenses. With benefits like 20% off on domestic flight bookings and up to 10% off on food orders on Zomato, users can save on their regular expenses and enjoy a range of discounts. Additionally, the monthly membership fee can be waived with a minimum spend, making it a smart choice for those who are looking to maximize their savings on their credit card usage.

Reward Points

  • The Standard Chartered Digi Smart Credit Card does not offer reward points on purchases.

  • But it provides discounts and cashback on select brands for movie, dining, and travel expenses. 

How to Apply for Standard Digi Smart Credit Card?

  • Go to the Standard Chartered Bank website.

  • Select the Digi Smart Credit Card.

  • Fill out the online application form with the details.

  • Upload the required documents.

  • Submit the application.

Before applying, it's important to fully understand the terms and conditions of the card. If a user still has any doubts or questions, reach out to the bank for clarification.

Standard Chartered Digi Smart Credit Card Eligibility

Applicants must be at least 18 years of age and have a steady source of income to be eligible for a credit card.

Documents Required

  • Proof of identity: PAN Card, Aadhaar card, Driver’s License, Passport, Voter’s ID, Overseas Citizen of India Card, Person of Indian Origin Card, Job card issued by NREGA, Letters issued by the UIDAI or any other government-approved photo ID proof.
  • Proof of Address: Aadhaar card, Driver’s License, Passport, Utility Bill not more than 3 months old, Ration Card, Property Registration Document, Person of Indian Origin Card, Job card issued by NREGA, Bank Account Statement, or any other government approved address proof.
  • Proof of Income: Latest one or two salary slips (not more than 3 months old), latest Form 16, and the last 3 months’ bank statement.

Frequently Asked Questions
1. How do I know how many reward points I have accumulated on my credit cards? How do I redeem them?

Visit the Standard Chartered bank’s website and follow the below steps to know the details of your accumulated reward points through:
Online Banking
Step 1: Log in to Online Banking
Step 2: Select “Online Rewards”
Step 3: Know the reward points accumulated on your credit card
Step 4: Follow the instructions to redeem your reward points
Mobile Banking
Step 1: Log in to SC Mobile
Step 2: Select “Credit Card Rewards” from the menu displayed on the left
Step 3: Know the reward points accumulated on your credit card
Step 4: Follow the instructions to redeem reward points.

2. How do I apply for E-statements?

Follow the below steps to apply for eStatement:
Step 1: Log in to Online Banking
Step 2: Select “eStatements and eAdvices”
Step 3: Go to “About Statements” and click “Sign Up Now”

3. How do I apply for a Standard Chartered Digi credit card?

  • Follow the below steps to apply for a credit card
    Step 1:  Visit the bank’s official website and select the card of your choice.
    Step 2: Select  “Apply Now” from the left hand side of the page
    Step 3: Provide information
    Step 4: Apply online

  • The issuance of credit cards is subject to Bank’s internal policy and verification checks.

4. How do I block lost or stolen credit cards?

Follow the below steps to report a lost or stolen credit card:
Step 1: Log in to Online Banking or SC Mobile
Step 2: Select “Help & Services”
Step 3: Go to “Card Management” and select “Report Lost/Stolen Card”
Step 4: Follow the instructions on the screen
Step 5: Upon completion, your card will be blocked and a replacement card will be sent to your registered mailing address

5. How can I know the available cash limit on Standard Chartered Digi Smart credit cards?

A portion of your credit card limit assigned to your credit card can be used to withdraw cash. The withdrawal of cash limit is subject to interest charges and cash withdrawal fee. To know  about interest charges and cash withdrawal fee please firstly you visit the bank’s website and then follow the below steps to know your cash limit through:
Online Banking
Step 1: Log in to Online Banking
Step 2: From left hand side menu, click on “Cards”
Step 3: Select “Card Details” to view the cash limit assigned under “Available Cash Advance Limit”
SC Mobile
Step 1: Log in to SC Mobile
Step 2: Select the credit card
Step 3: Go to “Card Details” on the top right section and view the “Available Cash Advance Limit” on your credit card

 

6. How can a customer check the available credit limit on Standard Chartered Digi smart credit card?

Follow the below steps to know the available credit limit on your credit card through:

  • Online Banking
    Step 1: Log in to Online Banking
    Step 2: From left-hand side menu, click on “Cards” and select “Card Summary”
    Step 3: Select “Card Details” to view the credit limit under the “Available Credit Limit”

  • SC Mobile
    Step 1: Log in to SC Mobile
    Step 2: Select the credit card
    Step 3: Go to “Card Details” on the top-right section and view the available credit limit on your credit card
