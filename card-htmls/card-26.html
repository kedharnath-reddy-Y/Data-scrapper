HDFC Bank Millennia Credit Card Review

If you're an avid online shopper on Amazon, Flipkart, or Myntra, love to spend online, and frequently travel within India, the HDFC Millennia Credit Card will be the ideal card for you. It offers amazing incentives and benefits, focusing on shopping and travel cashback. 

For those new to the credit card scene, the HDFC Millennia is a must-have in your wallet! However, if you want to earn cashback beyond the selected merchants, you can consider exploring other credit cards. Here are some alternatives:

Who should get an HDFC Bank Millennia Credit Card?

As the name implies, the HDFC Millennia card is one for young professionals looking to start their credit adventure and create a good credit profile. With its target clients in mind, HDFC Bank provides additional perks while buying on partner websites like Amazon and Flipkart.

If a user purchases online often, particularly with the firms with whom HDFC Bank has partnered, they should consider acquiring this card. Furthermore, the yearly charge is inexpensive and may be waived with annual spending of ₹1,00,000 only, which is an additional benefit.

It is vital to note that the HDFC Millennia Card does not provide direct cashback; instead, users will earn Cash Points, which may be redeemed for statement credit in a 1:1 ratio. Though it has no bearing on the value of the advantages, users may evaluate their selections if redeeming reward points is a task.

How to apply for an HDFC Bank Millennia Credit Card?

To apply for HDFC Millennia Credit Card, follow the below steps:

Step 1: Visit select.finology.in and click on the 'Credit Card' tab.
Step 2: Click on Card Issuers, where you will see a list of banks that issue credit cards.

1. HDFC Bank Introduces Changes to Credit Card Rules Effective August 1

Summary of HDFC Bank's New Credit Card Rules Effective from August 1, 2024

HDFC Bank is introducing new rules and charges for its credit card customers starting August 1, 2024:

  • Rental Transactions: A 1% fee, capped at Rs 3,000 per transaction, will be applied when paying rent through platforms such as CRED, PayTM, Cheq, MobiKwik, Freecharge, etc.

  • Fuel Transactions:

    • Consumer Cards: No extra fees for transactions below Rs. 15,000. A 1% fee, capped at Rs 3,000, for transactions above Rs 15,000.
    • Business Cards: No additional fee for transactions below Rs. 30,000. A 1% fee, capped at Rs 3,000, for spending over Rs 30,000 per transaction.

  • International/Cross Currency Transactions: A 3.5% markup fee will be levied. The markup fee remains at 2% for premium cards such as Infinia, Diners Black, etc., and 2.5% for 6E Rewards Indigo XL.

  • Late Payment Fees have been revised as follows:

    • Rs 0 for outstanding < = Rs 100
    • Incremental fees ranging from Rs 100 to Rs 1,300 for outstanding amounts of Rs 101 to over Rs 50,000.

  • Rewards Redemption towards Statement Credit: A Rs 50 redemption fee applies, not applicable to premium cards like Infinia, Diners Black, etc.

  • Finance Charges: A finance charge of 3.75% per month is applicable if the full amount isn't paid, relevant to all transactions. For premium cards, the finance charge is 1.99% per month.

  • Easy-EMI Processing Fee: A charge of up to Rs 299 for availing the Easy-EMI option at any store.

All fees are subject to GST, and the merchant category codes are defined by the network (Visa, Mastercard, etc.), not HDFC Bank. Charges described do not apply to Pixel Credit cards, with only the Rental Transaction Charge applicable to Commercial Credit Cards.

2. HDFC Bank credit cards to charge fee for rent, education payments via third-party apps from August 1

HDFC Bank has announced some changes to their credit card fees and rewards structure, effective from August 1, 2024. Here's a summary of the key changes:

  • Changes in fees for high-value transactions:
    • A 1% fee (capped at Rs 3,000) applies to utility bill payments exceeding Rs 50,000 (Rs 75,000 for business cards) on consumer credit cards.
    • Fuel purchases above Rs 15,000 (Rs 30,000 for business cards) on consumer credit cards will incur a 1% fee (capped at Rs 3,000).
  • Revised rewards for Tata Neu cards: The cashback structure for UPI payments made using Tata Neu Infinity and Tata Neu Plus credit cards is changing. You'll earn more NeuCoins if you use the Tata Neu UPI ID compared to other UPI IDs.

Frequently Asked Questions
1. How can I apply for HDFC Millennia Credit Card?

Step 1: Choose the “Credit Card” tab on the HDFC Bank website.
Step 2: Choose "Get My Credit Card" from the menu.
Step 3: Fill in the necessary information.
Step 4: Pick the card of your choice (Millennia Credit Card).

2. What is the benefit of HDFC Millennia Credit Card?

The HDFC Millennia Credit Card's notable features include 5% Cashback on select retailers like Amazon, BookMyShow, Cult.fit, Flipkart, Myntra, Sony LIV, Swiggy, Tata CLiQ, Uber, and Zomato, as well as Rs 1,000 worth of gift cards on purchases of at least Rs 1,000,00 in each calendar quarter. Dineout partners with restaurants and offers up to a 20% discount. This card also waives the fuel surcharge.

3. What is the credit limit offered on HDFC Millennia Credit Card?

This credit card's available balance is entirely determined by your income and CIBIL Score and has no set limit. HDFC Bank routinely increases the credit limit based on your usage and repayment history. So, having a healthy credit utilisation percentage and a solid repayment history is essential. You will receive a high credit limit on this credit card if you have a CIBIL Score of 750 or higher and an income of Rs 35,000 monthly.
 

4. Who is eligible for a Millennia Credit Card? 

The Millennia Credit Card is available to people with a gross monthly income greater than Rs 35,000 a month.

5. Is the HDFC Millenia Credit Card free for lifetime?

The majority of credit cards technically have an annual fee. But, if you use your card frequently and meet the minimum expenditure, these fees are waived. Likewise, if the bank determines you're a credit-worthy customer, they might give you a lifetime free credit card with no initiation or yearly fees.

2. Access to 200+ upscale restaurants' 1+1 on buffet deal.
