1. HDFC Bank Introduces Changes to Credit Card Rules Effective August 1

Summary of HDFC Bank's New Credit Card Rules Effective from August 1, 2024

HDFC Bank is introducing new rules and charges for its credit card customers starting August 1, 2024:

  • Rental Transactions: A 1% fee, capped at Rs 3,000 per transaction, will be applied when paying rent through platforms such as CRED, PayTM, Cheq, MobiKwik, Freecharge, etc.

  • Fuel Transactions:

    • Consumer Cards: No extra fees for transactions below Rs. 15,000. A 1% fee, capped at Rs 3,000, for transactions above Rs 15,000.
    • Business Cards: No additional fee for transactions below Rs. 30,000. A 1% fee, capped at Rs 3,000, for spending over Rs 30,000 per transaction.

  • International/Cross Currency Transactions: A 3.5% markup fee will be levied. The markup fee remains at 2% for premium cards such as Infinia, Diners Black, etc., and 2.5% for 6E Rewards Indigo XL.

  • Late Payment Fees have been revised as follows:

    • Rs 0 for outstanding < = Rs 100
    • Incremental fees ranging from Rs 100 to Rs 1,300 for outstanding amounts of Rs 101 to over Rs 50,000.

  • Rewards Redemption towards Statement Credit: A Rs 50 redemption fee applies, not applicable to premium cards like Infinia, Diners Black, etc.

  • Finance Charges: A finance charge of 3.75% per month is applicable if the full amount isn't paid, relevant to all transactions. For premium cards, the finance charge is 1.99% per month.

  • Easy-EMI Processing Fee: A charge of up to Rs 299 for availing the Easy-EMI option at any store.

All fees are subject to GST, and the merchant category codes are defined by the network (Visa, Mastercard, etc.), not HDFC Bank. Charges described do not apply to Pixel Credit cards, with only the Rental Transaction Charge applicable to Commercial Credit Cards.

2. HDFC Bank credit cards to charge fee for rent, education payments via third-party apps from August 1

HDFC Bank has announced some changes to their credit card fees and rewards structure, effective from August 1, 2024. Here's a summary of the key changes:

  • Charges for using third-party apps: There will be a 1% fee (capped at Rs 3,000) for using third-party payment apps like CRED, Paytm, etc. to pay rent or educational expenses with your HDFC credit card.
  • Changes in fees for high-value transactions:
    • A 1% fee (capped at Rs 3,000) applies to utility bill payments exceeding Rs 50,000 (Rs 75,000 for business cards) on consumer credit cards.
    • Fuel purchases above Rs 15,000 (Rs 30,000 for business cards) on consumer credit cards will incur a 1% fee (capped at Rs 3,000).
  • Revised rewards for Tata Neu cards: The cashback structure for UPI payments made using Tata Neu Infinity and Tata Neu Plus credit cards is changing. You'll earn more NeuCoins if you use the Tata Neu UPI ID compared to other UPI IDs.

Frequently Asked Questions
1. What is the HDFC Regalia Gold credit card, and how does it differ from other HDFC credit cards?

The HDFC Regalia Gold credit card is a premium credit card that offers a host of benefits to its cardholders, including:

  • Complimentary Club Vistara Silver Tier membership and MMT Black Elite membership
  • 5X reward points on spends at Marks & Spencer, Myntra, Nykaa, and Reliance Digital
  • Complimentary lounge access at over 1000 airports
  • 4 reward points on every Rs.150 spent (On retail* spends)
  • 1% cashback on all international transactions (upto Rs. 1,000/- per statement cycle)
  • Rs. 2,000/- worth Yatra voucher once you accumulate International spends of Rs. 1,50,000/- in a year
  • 500 bonus reward points on first utility bill payment
  • 2% cashback on all Smart Pay payments with a max cap of Rs 200 and reversal of Rs. 10
  • 1% cashback on all utility bill payments directly made to the bill merchant with a max cap of Rs 150

The HDFC Regalia Gold credit card differs from other HDFC credit cards in the following ways:

  • It offers a higher reward rate on spends at select merchants
  • It offers complimentary lounge access at over 1000 airports
  • It offers a higher cashback rate on international transactions
  • It offers a welcome benefit of complimentary Club Vistara Silver Tier membership and MMT Black Elite membership
2. What is the annual fee for the HDFC Regalia Gold credit card, and are there any renewal charges?

The annual fee for the HDFC Regalia Gold credit card is Rs. 2500 plus taxes. However, the annual fee can be waived if you spend Rs. 4 lakhs in an anniversary year (12 billing cycles).

3. What is the foreign currency markup fee for international transactions with this credit card?

The foreign currency markup fee for international transactions with the HDFC Regalia Gold credit card is 2%. This means that for every Rs.100 you spend in foreign currency, you will be charged an additional Rs.2.

5. How do I upgrade my existing HDFC credit card to the HDFC Regalia Gold credit card?

To upgrade your existing HDFC credit card to the HDFC Regalia Gold credit card, you can follow these steps:

  1. Call the HDFC Bank customer care number and request an upgrade.
  2. You will need to provide your name, account number, and the credit card number of your existing card.
  3. The customer care executive will verify your eligibility for the upgrade.
  4. If you are eligible, you will be asked to submit some documents, such as a copy of your PAN card and latest salary slip.
  5. Once the documents are submitted, the upgrade process will be initiated.
  6. The upgraded card will be sent to you within a few weeks.

7. How do I redeem the reward points earned with this card, and what are the redemption options available?

You can redeem your reward points earned with the HDFC Regalia Gold credit card through the following options:

  • Online redemption: You can redeem your reward points online through the HDFC Bank website or mobile app. You can redeem your points for flights, hotels, gift cards, and merchandise. The redemption value of your reward points is as follows:
  • Flights and hotel bookings via Smartbuy: 1 reward point = Rs. 0.5
  • Air miles conversion through Netbanking or SmartBuy: 1RP = upto 0.5 airmiles
  • Against products and vouchers via Netbanking or SmartBuy: 1 reward point = Up to Rs. 0.35
  • Against cashback: 1 reward point = Rs. 0.20
  • SmartBuy: You can redeem your reward points for flights and hotel bookings through the SmartBuy portal. You can also redeem your points for merchandise through the SmartBuy catalogue.
  • Statement credit: You can redeem your reward points for a statement credit against your outstanding balance. The redemption value of your reward points is as follows:
  • 1 reward point = Rs. 0.20
  • Gift cards: You can redeem your reward points for gift cards from a variety of merchants. The redemption value of your reward points will vary depending on the merchant.
  • Merchandise: You can redeem your reward points for merchandise from the HDFC Bank catalogue. The redemption value of your reward points will vary depending on the item.

Here are the steps on how to redeem your reward points online:

  1. Go to the HDFC Bank website and log in to your account.
  2. Click on the "Reward Points" tab.
  3. Select the redemption option you want to use.
  4. Enter the number of reward points you want to redeem.
  5. Review the redemption details and click on the "Redeem" button.
